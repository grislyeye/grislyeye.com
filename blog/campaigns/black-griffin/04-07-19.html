This is the second campaign diary for my 5th Edition D&D playtest campaign, Black Griffin. Black Griffin is a play-by-chat game that runs every week on Roll20. The party are mercenaries in the employ of the infamous Company of the Black Griffin. Lowly even for sellswords, they have been placed on punishment duty as company "fetches": bounty hunters charged with capture of deserters for trial.

The below is an edited log from of our in-character chat channel. We've added annotations so you can follow what's going on OOC.

Please enjoy!

The fish broth Biron prepares goes down easier than the seagull. And guards against the chilling night are creeping into the cave. You all feel the warmth of a good meal sitting in your guts as the weariness of your limbs commands attention.

Quinn
lays on his back on the sand, one hand on his full belly and drifts off to sleep.
Orin

shouts to the others:

Biron

looks slightly surprised by this.

Biron seems to fall asleep immediately, as is his manner. Soon followed by a weary Quinn. And so Orin's watch begins.

It is roughly midnight when Orin wakes Biron for his watch, nothing untoward other than flocks of birds having troubled their watch.

And so, in the pitch of night Biron wakes Quinn for the last watch before falling dead asleep again.

Quinn
grunts and dons his armour then slumps down in the low entryway with his bow over his lap.

At first much seems quiet. Gulls screech. The waves crash. It is peaceful, if cold.

Then you hear... something.

At first Quinn think it is then wind but as the sound gets louder... yes... it is clear... someone whistling a jaunty tune!

Quinn

crawls back into the cave and shakes Biron, then Orin.

Wake up, someone comes!

The pair wake with a start, a little weary to be startled at such an hour.

Biron
wakes eyes wide and whips up to a (very tight) crouch position.
Orin

grabs their bow and arrow, sneaking up to the edges of the cave, watching.

Mark rolled a Perception: 20.

The tide is still high, but with the little light from the embers of your fire you can see the narrow beach is empty.

gestures to Orin to pull back a lil.

The sound of whistling is getting closer... and higher... it appears to be coming from the cliffs above: someone walking along the coastal path?

But who would be travelling at this hour?

Biron

grins apologetically.

Well, sounded like a person, could be the wind whistling through the nooks and crinnies of the cliff some though...

Biron cautiously climbs the rocks leading back up to the path, quickly and quietly for a man of his size.

Biron
stays low and stays close to the rock face.

Coming along the coastal path you spy what appears to be a man wearing a tricorn hat. He carries a lantern hanging from the end of a long pole.

However, it is not him whistling!!

Upon his shoulder sits a black crow, whistling a tune like any human might.

Biron
crouches low to the ground and behind some rocks and bushes and keeps an eye on them.

As he approaches you can see the man is clearly much enjoying his stroll. He swings his arm and steps forward as though without a care in the world, in tune to the crow's whistling.

A nasty looking mace hangs from his belt, but then who wouldn't carry a weapon in such a remote spot as this?

Biron
looks for any tattoos, symbols or markings on the stranger or his weapons.

Nothing else untoward. He bears leather armour, and a coat that has seen better days. Its pockets jangle, perhaps with coin?

Biron
lays in waits. Looks down back to Orin to see how far away they are.

The man with the tricorn hat continues to pass by without a fuss, the whistling and light dimming into the distance.

Biron waits a while before he moves... but not before he catches a glimpse of something else moving down the path: four large folks, brandishing maces follow close behind, with obvious ill intent!

They do not appear to have seen you, but are following the man along the path.

Biron
stays silent and watches.

The four men pass by, treading softly and carefully after the man with the crow.

They soon disappear into the night as well.

Biron
moves back to the cave.

Nothin we need worry about.
