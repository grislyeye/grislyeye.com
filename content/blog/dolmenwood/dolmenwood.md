_Dolmenwood_ from Necrotic Gnome might be the apotheosis of the OSR. It really doesn't get more sandboxy than this. The setting just brims with player agency. There are no quantum ogres lurking in the woods here. No narrative plots waiting to ensnare players in tiresome railroads.

And the whimsy. So much whimsy.

The only problem with _Dolmenwood_ that I can think of is it might be _too_ perfect. Such flawless execution could only be tainted by having player characters coarsely stumble around its woods

Or, perhaps, it's too complete. It defies the DIY aspects of the OSR because, well, there is nothing left to do except play it, leaving the GM twidling his thumbs between sessions. The only ommission I can think of is a lack of mapped dungeons, a problem solved by the suite of adventures published in parallel with the core set.

One might also ask, who is this for? Its steep price (print Â£94) puts it out of reach of your casual player. It's for the fans, a product of the crowdfunding craze that puts lengthy books in the hands of diehards and collectors. No shade, this will sit on my shelves as testament to my lack of self-control. And why not? It's a work of art, something to be admired and an inspiration.
