Most of the non-human races in the Player's Handbook are based on Tolkien's works. The fey as presented here are meant as Pre-Tolkien variants on elves, dwarves, halflings and gnomes for low-magic campaigns. They are based on legends and fairytales (with a bit of artistic license). This preview includes the Fair Folk subrace and other fey subraces will feature in future posts.

Age.
As spirits, fey are not born and do not age in the same way mortals do. They live forever unless physical harm kills them or they are forgotten and fade away. Time works strangely in the feywild. A fey entering the lands of men might do so as a wide-eyed innocent, despite being thousands of (human) years old.
Alignment.
Capricious, contrary and whimsical, most fey lean towards chaos. Their natures can also lean towards spite (evil), innocence (good), apathy to mortal concerns (neutral) or even all things at once.
Ability Score Increase.
Your Dexterity score increases by 2.
Darkvision.
As spirits of the darkest parts of the woods and deepest caves, all fey have superior vision in dark and dim conditions. You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light.
Fairy Charm.
You have a magical gift. Choose one of the following:
Glamour.
You can cast the cantrip friends at will without material components. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.
Trickster's Knack.
You can cast the cantrip minor illusion at will and without material components. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.
Will-o'-the-wisp.
You can cast the cantrip dancing lights at will and without material components. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for it.
Fey Cunning.
You have advantage on all Intelligence, Wisdom and Charisma saving throws against magic.
Ban.
As spirits, you are compelled by religious proscriptions and ancient superstitions. These bans can be ignored, but to do so takes a toll. If a you choose to ignore a ban, you must make a Wisdom saving throw or take 1d6 psychic damage. Bans can also cause other complications, such as revealing your fey nature. Roll on the Bans table to find your ban.
Seeming.
You have a seeming: a magical illusion that allows you to appear as human. You can adopt or dismiss your seeming as a reaction. While in your seeming you appear as normal human without your tells. Albeit a short human, if you're one of the Little People. While a seeming hides your fey nature, it is not a disguise and you are instantly recognisable as the same person in either form, unless otherwise disguised.
Language.
You can speak, read, and write Common and Sylvan.
Subrace.
The fey are divided into two subraces: the Fair Folk and the Little People. Choose one of these subraces.

The Fair Folk

Sometimes called elves or sidhe, the Fair Folk are the minor nobility of the fey, second only to the archfey. Fair folk typically dwell deep in the forests, holding court in secluded glenns or by babbling brooks. Graceful and haughty, with an unearthly beauty, the Fair Folk consider themselves to be superior to humans and other fey.

Ability Score Increase.
Your Charisma score increases by 1.
Size.
The Fair Folk range from under 5 to over 6 feet tall and have slender builds. Your size is Medium.
Speed.
Your base walking speed is 35 feet.
Fair Folk Tells.
No two fey are quite alike and each is uniquely touched with aspects of plants, animals or elements. Roll for 2 tells from the Fair Folk Tells table below, or pick two tells using the example tells as inspiration. Tells do not have any game benefits.
Gift of the Woods.
You can cast the spell druidcraft at will.
Fair Folk Tells
d8 Tell
1 Hair like feathers or leaves.
2 Horns or antlers.
3 Green, light blue or lilac skin
4 Legs and cloven feet like a deer.
5 Delicate wings like a beetle or butterfly.
6 Translucent skin like glass or water.
7 Eyes like a wild cat
8 Skin the texture and colour of tree bark.
